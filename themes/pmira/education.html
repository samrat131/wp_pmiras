Why Precious Metals Should be a Part of Your Savings

There’s no denying the power of precious metals as a proven wealth facilitator. After all, during times of economic instability, they act as a safety net to not only preserve one’s wealth but also safeguard against financial uncertainty.

Since precious metals are not controlled by financial or government institutions like paper currency, they boast inherent value. As an investor, you get to enjoy unique benefits.

Invest in Precious Metals IRAs – Benefits

Just a few of the immediate benefits of investing in Precious Metal IRAs are:

Portfolio Diversification

Every educated investor knows that one of the basic rules of thumb is to diversify assets. Sticking to paper-backed assets such as stocks or mutual funds in today’s globalized economy is a mistake. Diversifying your portfolio through precious metals means you are spreading out risk while cutting it down significantly on a whole.

Added Wealth Security

The value of paper assets like stocks is directly linked with the misgivings that take place at Wall Street and Washington. However, precious metals are not tied to the same “blunders” and offer added wealth security, particularly if your portfolio is diversified with gold and silver.

Privacy

When you purchase precious metals, your ownership is completely private, unlike other asset types. Since these precious metals are in your possession only, no bank, government or financial institution will be eyeing your portfolio.

Protection from a Sagging Economy

Precious metals, including gold and silver, offer the best protection against economic and political uncertainty. A stock market crash is quite likely and the US dollar continues to decline. You, however, have nothing to worry about.

Guard against Inflation

The US dollar continues to sink but Americans have been quick to preserve their future purchasing power by investing in precious metals like gold, since it acts as a safe haven against inflation. The Federal Reserve continues to print loads of money and due to the declining US dollar, gold’s value has gone up even more.

Liquidity

Precious metals like gold bullion are valued solely by weight and metal content – this makes them extremely tangible and liquid assets that are not affected by investment woes like other instruments are (stocks, bonds or real estate, for example). Precious metals are, in fact, one of the most “liquidable” assets you could possible invest in. You also have full access after buying them.

Precious Metal Types

Precious metals as an investment instrument generally refer to gold, silver, palladium or platinum. All offer unique benefits and can either be kept under your possession after purchase or placed in a Precious Metals IRA retirement account.

Here’s how they are used:

Gold

Dating back to medieval times, gold has always been in demand and is considered an insignia of wealth and power.

Uncertainty in the global economy and a declining US dollar makes gold an excellent long-term investment.

Platinum and Palladium

Both these precious metals are characterized by their grey/white hue and catalytic properties. They have industrial applications similar to gold and silver, including jewelry.

Silver

Silver, much like gold, has also been used for many generations as a symbol of wealth, having applications in coinage and jewelry, to name a few.

Silver’s electrical conductivity, malleability and widespread use in industrial and manufacturing applications make it a great precious metal investment.
